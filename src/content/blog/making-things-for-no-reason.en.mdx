Most of the things I'm proudest of started with no plan and no point. A cocktail
named after a boss fight. A WoW character render fetched at build time just so a
card on my About page shows my current gear. This blog, which exists mostly
because I wanted somewhere to think out loud.

## The case for pointless projects

When a thing has to ship, every decision gets argued down to what's reasonable.
Pointless projects don't have that gravity. You can spend an afternoon making a
recipe page feel *cinematic* and nobody will ask you to justify the budget.

A few things I've learned letting myself build for no reason:

- **Constraints you choose are fun; constraints you're handed are work.** Same
  constraint, completely different feeling.
- **Polish is a love language.** The 3am detail nobody notices is the whole point.
- **Finishing is optional, but starting isn't.** Half my favorite things are
  unfinished. They still did their job.

> The reward for a hobby is the hobby.
